Poison Oak Symptoms and Treatments

Symptoms

The rash of poison oak is characterized by red, weeping blisters and severe itching. The rash appears within 8 hours to three days of contact with the plant oil Urushiol. There are people who claim to be immune to it, over time the more you come in to contact you will become allergic due to the fact that it builds up in your bloodstream.

Quick Tips
  • Wash with warm water and soap. Washing within 10mins of exposure can reduce the chance of allergic reaction.
  • Reduce the chance of spreading. Remove and wash anything that may have come in contact with oils (clothing, tools, etc)
  • Do not burn the plant. Inhaling smoke from poison oak can irritate and harm you lungs and nasal passage.
  • Prevention is key. Look around the site and learn how to spot posion oak!

Treatments

The way to treat poison oak if you know that you have come in to contact is to wash affected area within 10 minutes. If more time has passed, use Tecnu poison oak soap available at most drug stores. To ease discomfort, use zinc oxide, Benadryl or Calamine lotion. Tecnu also makes a product named Calagel.
