Poison Oak Field Guide

Spring

In spring the leaves have a bold green appearance with small white flowers. The flowers emit a faint smell reminiscent of solvent.

Summer

In summer it turns a waxy red and can be easy to spot, as the leaves become waxy with the poison oil urushiol

Fall

In the fall the leaves turn brown as it starts to go dormant. Although the plant becomes dormant, the oils that cause the rash stick around all year long.

Winter

In the winter time there are no leaves as it goes dormant, just the vines are left. It can like an out of place dormant shrumb or a tumbleweed randomly laying dormant in the woods.

Gound Cover

It can grow small and low as a ground cover on the edge of paths and clearings

Shrub

It can grow in to small bush like bunches that mimic northwest shrubs.

Vine

It can find refuge in trees and head up to the canopy, creating huges vines that look very different than english ivy.

Mimicry

Here it is shown mimicing low growing plants, blackberry bushes and shrubs. It will literally morph shape to blend in with other plants.
