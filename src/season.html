Poison Oak Field Guide

Spring

Poison oak changes it's appearance throughout the season. In spring it has a bold green appearance with small white flowers. In summer it starts to turn a reddish color. In the fall it becomes brown. In the winter time the leaves recede and mostly the stalks remain. Even though the leaves aren't visible, it can still cause skin irritations.
